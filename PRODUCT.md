# Product

## Users
Non-technical knitters, primarily women 30–65, who buy patterns on Etsy or download from Ravelry. They love to knit but feel anxious or confused by standard abbreviations (k2tog, ssk, yo). Active in knitting groups and social communities. They share useful finds with friends.

## Brand
Woolery App — warm, approachable, genuinely useful. Not a developer tool. Feels like that one friend in the knitting circle who actually knows what all the abbreviations mean.

## Tone
Warm, plain-spoken, a little playful. Treats knitters as smart people who simply never memorized a notation system. Never condescending. Never corporate.

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
A free, no-account web tool that translates knitting pattern abbreviations into plain English (and back). Primary value: instant clarity for patterns that feel cryptic. Secondary: save to file, print, copy, custom abbreviations, PDF extraction.

## Anti-references
- Developer tools (Linear, Vercel, GitHub) — wrong register
- Generic SaaS purple gradient — the old state we're fixing
- Overly crafty grandmotherly aesthetics — not twee, not folksy clip art
- Millennial pink "girl boss" — dated

## Strategic principles
- First impression must show the transformation: cryptic → readable
- Viral loop: knitters share useful things with their groups; surface the share moment
- Free forever: no friction, no signup, trust immediately
- "Works with Etsy PDFs" is the most relatable hook for the target user
